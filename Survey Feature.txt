Implement the survey inside MemoLink as an “Evaluation Survey” module, not as a normal feedback form. Your current app already has feedback/bug report support for admin, but the assessment survey should be separate because it is for research data collection, not product support. Your existing docs already define feedback/bug reports for admin use, so mixing research survey answers into that table may confuse the evidence later.

Best approach:

Add a new in-app flow called:

MemoLink Evaluation Survey

Where to place it:

After the user completes the prototype task flow:

Create/upload a note
Ask a question using RAG
Check citation/source
Generate/review reminder or action item
Complete the survey

Good UI placement:

Add a button in the sidebar or Help menu:

“Take Evaluation Survey”

Or show it after completing a guided demo:

“Help improve MemoLink — complete evaluation survey”

Do not force it every time. Make it optional.

Recommended database tables:

survey_responses

Fields:

id
user_id nullable
workspace_id nullable
participant_code
role

created_at

survey_answers

Fields:

Why separate tables are better:

You can export the survey answers easily.
You can analyse Likert-scale answers separately from comments.
You avoid mixing research data with bug reports.
You can show evidence in Assessment 2.
You can keep participant identity optional.

This fits your capstone because your core evaluation should focus on knowledge capture, contextual retrieval, citations, reminders, usability, and productivity support — not every advanced feature. Your current project is already broad, so the in-app survey should evaluate only the core workflow.

Add one admin/export feature:

Admin can export survey results as CSV.

This will help you when writing Assessment 2 and Assessment 3.

Suggested API endpoints:

POST /api/v1/evaluation-survey/submit

GET /api/v1/admin/evaluation-survey

GET /api/v1/admin/evaluation-survey/export

Suggested feature flag:

evaluation_survey_enabled

Best wording for your report:

“An in-app evaluation survey will be implemented to collect structured qualitative and perception-based feedback after participants complete selected prototype tasks. The survey will use Likert-scale items and short-answer questions to evaluate knowledge capture, contextual retrieval, citation usefulness, reminder support, usability, trust, and privacy concerns. Survey responses will be stored separately from product bug reports and will be exported for thematic analysis and descriptive summary.”

My strict recommendation: implement it, but keep it simple. Do not build a complex survey builder. Hard-code one survey for the capstone evaluation and allow admin CSV export.
